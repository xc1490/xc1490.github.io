Decoding Imagined and Overt Speech from sEEG Neural Recordings

Beyond the representation of speech using physical and interpretable speech parameters. Recent speech recognition and synthesis models exploit the power of self-supervised learning. Wav2Vec and HuBERT models show their strengths by learning a quantized representation using masked transformers and con- trastive learning. Recent advances in neural prostheses and understanding the neural representation for speech have applied the HuBERT model and demon- strated successful speech decoding from the human brain. However, most of these successful decoding attempts rely on rare and expensive intracranial EEG (ECoG or Utah Array) recordings. Can we decode intelligible speech from more accessible sEEG recordings? In this study, we use a neural decoding framework to decode intelligible, imagined speech from patients implanted with sEEG ar- rays. Our results show success in decoding intelligible speech from a large pa- tient sEEG dataset with unprecedented accuracy.
