Decoding Imagined and Overt Speech from sEEG Neural Recordings

Advancing Neural Speech Processing Technology

Beyond the representation of speech using physical and interpretable speech parameters, recent speech recognition and synthesis models exploit the power of self-supervised learning. Wav2Vec and HuBERT models show their strengths by learning a quantized representation using masked transformers and contrastive learning.

Recent advances in neural prostheses and understanding the neural representation for speech have applied the HuBERT model and demonstrated successful speech decoding from the human brain. However, most of these successful decoding attempts rely on rare and expensive intracranial EEG (ECoG or Utah Array) recordings.

Can we decode intelligible speech from more accessible sEEG recordings? In this study, we use a neural decoding framework to decode intelligible, imagined speech from patients implanted with sEEG arrays. Our results show success in decoding intelligible speech from a large patient sEEG dataset with unprecedented accuracy.
